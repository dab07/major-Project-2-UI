ASL

American Sign Language

American Sign Language (ASL) is a natural language that serves as the predominant sign language of Deaf communities in the United States and most of Anglophone Canada. ASL is a visual language, distinct from spoken languages, with its own grammar and syntax. It consists of gestures, hand movements, and facial expressions to convey meaning. Learning ASL is important for fostering communication and inclusivity within the Deaf community and promoting understanding and accessibility for all individuals.
